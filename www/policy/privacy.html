Privacy Policy

Introduction

The GENI identity portal is a bridge between the InCommon Federation and GENI. If your portal account is approved, you will have access to GENI resources for research and experimentation. The GENI portal is operated by Raytheon BBN Technologies on behalf of the GENI Project Office and the National Science Foundation. Before sending confidential or personal information, you should understand that most materials on Raytheon BBN Technologies systems are subject to laws and policies that may compel Raytheon BBN Technologies to disclose them.

GENI uses your InCommon attributes to establish your identity within the GENI Federation. The GENI Federation consists of resource providers and services across the United States. The GENI portal may associate some of your InCommon asserted attributes with your GENI identity. These attributes may be used to

  • authorize you to use various GENI resources
  • uniquely identify your use of various GENI services
  • contact you in case of problems with your GENI resources, or your use of GENI services

Information We Collect

The following information may be collected when you use the GENI identity portal:

  • Your web browser type, operating system, and IP address from which you access the wiki.
  • The portal pages you access.
  • Any actions you take via portal pages.

The following information may be collected from your single sign-on Identity Provider:

  • Your eduPersonPrincipalName as asserted by the Identity Provider used by you during the single sign-on process. The eduPersonPrincipalName attribute is most likely a personally identifiable attribute that closely resembles your name and public persona.
  • Your email address as asserted by the Identity Provider used by you during the single sign-on process. The mail attribute is most likely a personally identifiable email address that can be used to contact you.
  • Your affiliation as asserted by the Identity Provider used by you during the single sign-on process. The affiliation attribute is not typically personally identifiable and indicates your association with your home institution. Common values are "member", "staff", "faculty", "student".
  • Your given name as asserted by the Identity Provider used by you during the single sign-on process. The givenName attribute is most likely your first name and can be a personally identifiable attribute.
  • Your surname as asserted by the Identity Provider used by you during the single sign-on process. The surname (sn) attribute is most likely your last name and can be a personally identifiable attribute.

How This Information is Used

Some of this information is logged to monitor performance and security of the site. Other information is stored and associated with your account for as long as your account is active. GENI may use this information as input when considering account requests. GENI may also use this information to contact you if there is a problem with some of the GENI resources you are using.

Information Shared with Partners

Some of the collected information is shared with other members of the GENI Federation. This includes GENI resource providers and GENI security staff during the investigation of a security incident. Some of this shared information may be derived from the information noted above as asserted by your Identity Provider, and may be personally identifiable.

Release of Information to Third Parties

None of the information is shared with third parties.
